Section 3 · Search and answer enginesWhat people type to reach us, and the one query worth explaining.

On CatholicMom, branded searches (our name) drove 52,924 clicks and discovery searches (not our name) 334,832, so 86% of search clicks come from people who were not looking for us. That discovery is what scales.

The query to explain: “free porn.” It is the second-largest discovery driver, 5,675 clicks on about 210,352 impressions at position 34. This is not a tracking error and not traffic to worry about. It lands on a real CatholicMom article, “Young Men Breaking Free: Tools to Combat Porn Addiction in Our Homes,” (/articles/2015/04/30/young-men-breaking-free-tools-to-combat-porn-addiction-in-our-homes). People searching for pornography are being met by a Catholic resource on breaking free from it. That is the mission working in the exact place it is needed. Worth doing: keep that article current and link today's help resources from it, so a search that starts in temptation ends somewhere genuinely helpful.

Answer engines sent 34 sessions to CatholicMom this week (chatgpt.com 31, perplexity 2, perplexity.ai 1), people who asked an AI and clicked through. Small today, but a real and growing channel; the move is to make our best content quotable so the machines cite us.
