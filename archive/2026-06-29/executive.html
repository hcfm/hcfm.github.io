Executive Summary

Father Fred Jenga, C.S.C., President · week of June 29 to 5, 2026 · data through Jul 5 (YouTube lags 2-3 days) · Emmanuel Epau, Analytics
This week the daily Rosary and Mass reached 11,587 engaged views on YouTube alone, in line with what is normal for this season, alongside steady engagement across Facebook and our websites. The ministry's digital presence is stable and healthy.
The standout this week: a Family Rosary reel on Facebook, 2026-06-29. It drew 66,021 engagements (59,919 reactions, 3,747 comments, 2,355 shares), which is 97 percent of everything that account earned on Facebook this week. “Today, the Father Peyton Center is honored to welcome pilgrims on the National Eucharistic Pilgrimag…” One moment traveling far, not a broad lift, so read the aggregate below with that in mind. Do more of this: the subject and format clearly landed; plan a follow-up for the audience it just reached, while attention is high.

Where the money is working

Paid ads on Family Rosary YouTube buy shallow views. In 2026, paid was 26% of raw views but only 29% of engaged views, because a paid view is often a swipe past rather than a watch. Across the websites, paid advertising moves only fatherpeyton.org (about a quarter of its traffic); everywhere else it is 0 to 1%. The organic levers, search and email, carry the estate.

Recommendation

The next opportunity is the Global Rosary in the fall, which we are preparing to measure fully, so that we can tell you not just how many watched, but how many joined, and from which countries.
